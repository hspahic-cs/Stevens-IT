Proof that Orthogonal Matrices Have Eigenvalues = 1

Recall that any Orthognal matrix has its inverse as its transpose.

Then Ox = lx, where O is the orthogonal matrix, x its eigenvector & l is lambda has the following property:

Ox = lx -> (xt)(Ot) = l(xt)

(xt)(Ot)Ox = l^2(xt)x

Since O is orthogonal:

(xt)x = l^2(xt)x -> ||x||^2 = l^2||x||^2

This can only happen when l is either 1 or -1 for all l. I'll leave the rest of the proof to the reader.
